# **Waste to Energy Carbon Methodology Process Map**

| Project Phase | Applicable Methodology | Project Activity | What’s Credited (tCO₂e) | Typical Credit Owner | Notes / Pitfalls |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Mechanical-Biological Treatment \- sorting (recyclables recovery) | AMS-III.AJ – Recovery & recycling of materials from solid wastes | Recover and recycle recyclable materials (e.g., plastics, metals, paper, glass) from waste streams that would otherwise end up in landfills, incinerators, or uncontrolled disposal. | Avoided emissions from production of virgin materials (metals, plastics, paper) Mechanical sorting is not the creditable activity. | MBT operator / recycler | Must ensure no double counting with downstream recyclers; leakage if transport is significant |
| Organics Anaerobic Digestion (AD) | ACM0022 – Alternative waste treatment processes (large-scale)  | waste diversion/landfill avoidance for organics and combustibles | Avoided landfill methane from organics/sludge; avoided emissions from fossil grid electricity by using biogas power/heat | AD plant operator | Must prove feedstock would otherwise be landfilled; control fugitive CH₄ from digester |
| Biogas utilization (CHP / biomethane) | ACM0022 |  | Additional credits for displacement of grid/fossil energy | AD/CHP operator | Avoid double counting with renewable energy policies (check baseline grid mix) |
| Refuse-Derived Fuel (RDF) production (from non-recyclables) | ACM0022 | Non-recyclable but high calorie (combustible) fractions (plastics, textiles, paper residues) are isolated for RDF and sold to cement plant as fuel substitution (co-firing / partial substitution) | Avoided landfill methane from combustible fraction diverted to RDF | MBT/RDF operator | Only credits the diversion from landfill, not combustion benefits |
| RDF combustion in cement plant | ACM0003 – Partial substitution of fossil fuels in cement manufacture |  | Avoided fossil CO₂ from displaced coal/petcoke | Cement plant operator (unless contractually transferred) | Double counting risk: landfill avoidance credited under ACM0022, fuel substitution under ACM0003 – need clear credit ownership |
| Optional: | VMR0044 \- Biochar from digestate thermochemically stablized | Organic digestate from Anaerobic digestion could be pyrolyzed to produce biochar | Carbon removals  | MBT/RDF operator | Need to ensure materials are on positive list.  |

# **Example Waste to Energy Projects**

**Project Name:** Taiping Township Waste Resource Utilization Project  
**Country/Region:** Heilongjiang Province, China  
**Project type:** Energy industries (renewable/non-renewable sources); Waste handling and disposal  
**Methodology:** ACM0022  
**Status:** Registered  
**Estimated Annual Credits:** 28,578  
**Description**: The proposed project is a new plant which is designed to treat organic wastes through anaerobic digestion and composting to produce the natural gas and organic fertilizers. The natural gas produced will be used for distribution in a natural gas distribution grid, and organic fertilizers will be transported to the local planting market. The proposed project uses organic wastes such as straw and livestock manure as raw materials. It is expected to treat 34,500 tons of straw and 69,000 tons of livestock manure annually, producing 3,777,800 Nm3 of natural gas and 15,769.95 tons of solid bio-organic fertilizers. The project activities include waste pretreatment unit, anaerobic digesters unit, biogas purification unit, and organic fertilizer production workshop

**Project Name:** Guangxi Guigang Food Waste Recycling Project  
**Country/Region:** Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, China  
**Project type:** Energy industries (renewable/non-renewable sources); Waste handling and disposal  
**Methodology:** ACM0022  
**Status:** Registered  
**Estimated Annual Credits:** 26,194  
**Description**: The purpose of the project is to treat MSW with anaerobic digesters and utilize recovered biogas for electricity generation. The project activity includes MSW collection, MSW pre-treatment system, anaerobic digester, biogas purification, biogas utilization system and emergency flaring system. The project is to utilize the biogas produced by anaerobic digestion for power generation. The electricity generated by the project is first used for the project and the surplus will be supplied to China South Power Grid (CSPG) which is dominated by coal-fired power stations.

**Project Name:** Bac Ninh Province Domestic Waste Power Generation Project  
**Country/Region:** Bac Ninh, Vietnam  
**Project type:** Energy industries (renewable/non-renewable sources); Waste handling and disposal  
**Methodology:** ACM0022  
**Status**: Under Validation  
**Estimated Annual Credits:** 32,337  
**Description:** Bac Ninh Province Domestic Waste Power Generation Project (hereinafter referred to as the Project) is located at Bac Ninh Province , Vietnam. The Project is owned and implemented by Bac Ninh EU \- Conch Venture new energy Co., Ltd. which is owned by China Conch Venture Holdings (HK) Limited . The project is established for the purpose of treatment of municipal solid waste (“MSW”) with energy recovery and electricity generation and no heat recovered for thermal generation is involved. The project is designed to dispose of a daily average 300 tons of MSW through one incineration systems with the treatment capacity of 300 tons/d of MSW, matched with one set of 6 MW condensing turbine and one set of 6 MW turbo generators. The project will dispose of 109,500 tons of MSW every year.

**Project Name:** Guangzhou Eastern Solid Resource Recycling Center MSW Integrated treatment Plant Project  
**Country/Region:** Guangdong Province, China  
**Project type:** Energy industries (renewable/non-renewable sources); Waste handling and disposal  
**Methodology:** ACM0022  
**Status**: Registered  
**Estimated Annual Credits: 359,014**  
**Description:** The purpose of the project is to treat MSW with anaerobic digesters and utilize recovered biogas for electricity generation. The project activity includes MSW collection, MSW pre-treatment system, biodiesel production system, anaerobic digester, biogas purification, electricity generation system and waste heat recovery. The fresh waste of the project is mainly kitchen and catering waste of the Guangzhou City and the design processing capacity of the project for the fresh waste is 2,040 tonnes per day. The biogas from anaerobic digestion will be used for electricity generation with internal combustion generators. The total installed capacity will be 10.34 MW included 4 generators with each 1.063 MW and 2 generators with each 3.044 MW. Before the implementation of the project activity, the fresh waste would be treated in a landfill site with a partial capture of the landfill gas (LFG) and flaring of the captured LFG. The project is to utilize the biogas produced by anaerobic digestion for power generation. The electricity generated by the project is supplied to China South Power Grid (CSPG) which is dominated by coal-fired power stations. In absence of the project, equivalent amount of electricity exported to the grid by the project would have otherwise been supplied by China South Power Grid (CSPG).
