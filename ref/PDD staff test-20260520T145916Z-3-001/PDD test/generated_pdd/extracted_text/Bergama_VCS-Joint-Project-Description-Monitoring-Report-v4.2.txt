1.1 Summary Description of the Project 
The project, Bergama Integrated Solid Waste Ma nagement Facility and Electricity Generation 
Facility (10.157 MWm / 9.898 MWe / 13.895) is in Izmir Province, Bergama District, Ka şıkçı 
Neighborhood, and owned and operated by İzmir Doğu Star Elektrik Üretim A. Ş.. 
The project site is located within the boundari es of the II. Class Bergama Solid Waste Landfill 
Facility. It is planned to integrate the facilities to be established within the scope of the project 
and the existing II. Class Solid Waste Landfill Facility. 
Waste acceptance to the sanitary landfill started in 2011. The facility has two lots. Currently, 
only municipal wastes from Bergama and Dikili dist ricts are disposed of with the regular landfill 
method in the mentioned facility. In the planned situation within the scope of the project, waste 
will be brought to the site from Be rgama District, Dikili District, K ınık District and Alia ğa District. 
It is planned that the most suitable system for the conversion of th e "Bergama Solid Waste 
Disposal Facility", which is operated by the Izmi r Metropolitan Municipality, into an Integrated 
Solid Waste Management Facility, to integrate the Mechanical Separation Facility, 
Biomethanization Facility, Compost Facility and Electricity Generation Facility. 
After the mixed municipal wastes coming to the fa cility are separated according to their types at 
the Mechanical Separation Facility, the biodegradable wastes will be taken to the 
Biomethanization Facility, the remaining wastes will  be sent to the existing sanitary landfill and 
the recyclable wastes will be sent to licensed companies. The biogas to be obtained from the 
Biomethanization Facility and the methane gas to be extracted from the sanitary landfill will be 
converted into electrical energy at the Electric ity Generation Facility and transferred to the 
interconnected system to generate income. In ad dition, the solid fermented product obtained in 
the Biomethanization Facility will be sent to the Compost Facility and can be offered to the 
market if the compost product to be obtained meets the standards. In this way, the waste will 
be stabilized, the amount of waste to be stored will be minimized, electricity and heat energy 
will be obtained and the negative effects of wastes on the environment will be eliminated. 
In line with the sustainable integrated wast e management planned within the scope of the 
project, the packaging wastes to be collected se parately at the source will be sent to the 
collection and sorting facilities  licensed by the Ministry of Environment and Urbanization, and 
they will be sorted according to their types there. Packaging wastes to be collected separately 
at their source will not be accepted to the facility or any sanitary landfill. 
Estimated electricity generation and correspo nding annual estimated emission reduction 
values are 74,235 MWh and 76,729 tCO2 respectively. The starting date of the project activity

1.11 Description of the Project Activity 
Mixed municipal wastes collected from the projec t area will be brought to the waste reception 
unit of the Facility and after all of them enter the Mechanical Separation Facility, they will be 
separated as biodegradable waste, residual waste and waste suitable for recycling. 
Some of the biodegradable waste will be sent to the Biomethanization Facility, and some of it 
will be sent to the Sanitary Landfill Area within the scope of the Temporary Article 1 of the 
Regulation on Regular Storage of Wastes, which was published in the Official Gazette dated 
26.03.2010 and numbered 27533.  
Portion of the waste suitable for recovery will be sent to licensed disposal facilities and will 
contribute to the economy. 
Remaining waste will be sent to the Landfill for disposal. 
The capacity of the Mechanical Separation Facility to be established within the scope of the 
project has been determined as 435 tons/day, taking into account the total waste generation 
amount to be generated according to the population in the target year 2035. The units to be 
found in the system are given below. It will be a fully automatic system without manual 
separation. 
 Waste Reception Area 
 Bag Opener 
 Conveyors 
 Trommel Sieve 
 Magnetic Separator 
 Optical Separator 
 Ballistic Separator 
 Bale Press 
 Product/Waste Accumulation Area (The area where the waste bales pressed in the bale 
press machine are stored) 
 Product Warehouse (Biode gradable wastes and residual wast es will be collected in closed 
trailers that will be constantly present)

The biodegradable wastes from the Mechanical Separation Facility will come to the 
Biomethanization Facility, where there will be 27.30% solid fermented product, 36.35% liquid 
fermented product and 36.35% decomposition loss. 
A part of the liquid fermented product formed in the Biomethanization Facility will be 
transferred back to the system, the remaining port ion will be taken to the balancing pool and a 
treatment plant in order to meet the conditions for the discharge of water into the sewer system 
in accordance with the provisions of the Water Pollution Control Regulation and the IZSU 
Wastewater Discharge into the Sewage Network.  
Approximately 75% of the liquid fermented product can be returned to the system. The 
remaining 25% will be transferred to the treatment plant to be established within the scope of 
the project. The treatment plant to be established will be established in accordance with the 
technology that can purify the liquid fermented product. 
The solid fermented product formed in the Biomet hanization Facility will be directed to the 
Compost Facility, where compost product and non-standard compost will be obtained. The 
compost product coming out of the Compost Facility  will be offered to the market in order to be 
used as fertilizer if it meets the Compost Communiqué, Annex-2 Compost Quality Parameters 
and Annex-3 Stability Parameters, published in the Official Gazette dated 05.03.2015 and 
numbered 29286. Regarding the placing of compost on the market, the opinion will be taken 
from the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry. If the compost product from the Compost Facility 
cannot be supplied to the market, it will be disposed of by storing in the sanitary landfill in the 
appropriate class to be determined according to  the analysis result. Non-standard compost will 
be disposed of by storage in the existing Landfill. 
The Biomethanization Facility will have a total ca pacity of 228 tons/day, where 208 tons/day of 
biodegradable waste calculated for the target year within the scope of the project and 
biodegradable waste that can be taken from outside according to the conditions of the day will 
be processed. The final capacity of the Biomethanization Facility  will be reached by increasing 
the number of years. A total of 3 reactors with a capacity of 130 tons/reactor (3.512

facility, 1 reactor will be commissioned for the year 2020; A total of 2 reactors will be

1 more reactor for 2035.

 Sustainable Development Contributions Activity Description

The Project will contribute to sustainabl e development in the following ways:  
 Reduce CH 4 emission. The project will recover and destroy landfill gas that consists 
mainly of greenhouse gas methane and would otherwise be released directly into the 
atmosphere, effectively reducing greenhouse  gas emissions, which contributes to the 
Turkey’s commitment to carbon dioxide emissions;  
 Increase electricity production from renewable sources, which will certainly reduce the 
consumption of fossil fuel in Turkish Power Grid and further help to achieve the national 
action to promote renewable energy development;  
 Improve the operational safety of the landfills. The project reduces the potential safety 
hazards of landfill gas explosion by recycling landfill gas and improves the safety of 
landfill operation;  
 The project will also provide new jobs opport unities and increase tax revenue, which will 
have a positive effect on the local economy.  
 Improving air quality (i.e. by reducing odor) and therefore having positive effects on the 
local environment.  
Nevertheless, it is still possible to note that the project will make positive contributions to at least 
four Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). These are:  
SDG Goal 3: Good Health and Well-being: The proposed project acti vity will reduce GHG 
emissions, methane in particular, which is directly related to harmful effect of bad air quality. By 
implying this project air pollution will be decrease and reduce the number of deaths and illnesses 
from hazardous chemicals and air, the proposed Project will improve air quality.  
SDG Goal 7: Affordable and Clean Energy: The proposed Project is a waste to power project that 
will generate renewable energy by capturing methane from municipal waste and utilizing it to 
produce thermal and electric energy through gas engine systems. By supplying renewable energy 
generated at the plant to the national grid, the pr oposed Project will contribute to increasing the 
share of renewable energy in the global energy mix and the proportion of the population with 
primary reliance on clean fuels and technology.  
SDG Goal 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth : The demand for food and electric energy is 
rapidly increasing in Turkey for various reasons, such as industrialization, urbanization, economic 
development, and population growth. The country's external dependence on agricultural products 
has intensified because the increasing demand cannot be met by a decreasing domestic 
agricultural production capacity which is due to several reasons, such as shrinkage of agricultural 
land, increasing migration to urban spaces from rural areas where agricultural production is 
densely located, and dramatic increase in the cost of inputs for agricultural production. 
Therewithal, Turkey, which cannot meet its increasing electricity demand due to the deprivation 
of conventional resources used to generate energy, such as coal, oil, and natural gas, has also 
become foreign-dependent on energy. Through its implementation, the proposed project activity 
will contribute to reducing Turkey's foreign dependency by generating renewable energy out of

the proposed project activity will achieve higher levels of economic productivity; hence, increasing

SDG Goal 13: Climate Action : The proposed project activity will reduce GHG emissions by

4 IMPLEMENTATION STATUS 
4.1 Implementation Status of the Project Activity 
 
The project, Bergama Integrated Solid Waste Ma nagement Facility and Electricity Generation 
Facility (10.157 MWm / 9.898 MWe / 13.895) is in Izmir Province, Bergama District, Ka şıkçı 
Neighborhood, and owned and operated by İzmir Doğu Star Elektrik Üretim A. Ş.. 
The project site is located within the boundari es of the II. Class Bergama Solid Waste Landfill 
Facility. It is planned to integrate the facilities to be established within the scope of the project 
and the existing II. Class Solid Waste Landfill Facility. 
Waste acceptance to the sanitary landfill started in 2011. The facility has two lots. Currently, 
only municipal wastes from Bergama and Dikili dist ricts are disposed of with the regular landfill 
method in the mentioned facility. In the planned situation within the scope of the project, waste 
will be brought to the site from Be rgama District, Dikili District, K ınık District and Alia ğa District. 
After the mixed municipal wastes coming to the fa cility are separated according to their types at 
the Mechanical Separation Facility, the biodegradable wastes will be taken to the 
Biomethanization Facility, the remaining wastes will  be sent to the existing sanitary landfill and 
the recyclable wastes will be sent to licensed companies. The biogas to be obtained from the 
Biomethanization Facility and the methane gas to be extracted from the sanitary landfill will be 


===== PAGE 35 =====
 Joint Project Description & Monitoring Report: VCS Version 4.2 
35 
converted into electrical energy at the Electric ity Generation Facility and transferred to the 
interconnected system to generate income. In ad dition, the solid fermented product obtained in 
the Biomethanization Facility will be sent to the Compost Facility and can be offered to the 
market if the compost product to be obtained meets the standards. In this way, the waste will 
be stabilized, the amount of waste to be stored will be minimized, electricity and heat energy 
will be obtained and the negative effects of wastes on the environment will be eliminated. 
In line with the sustainable integrated wast e management planned within the scope of the 
project, the packaging wastes to be collected se parately at the source will be sent to the 
collection and sorting facilities  licensed by the Ministry of Environment and Urbanization, and 
they will be sorted according to their types there. Packaging wastes to be collected separately 
at their source will not be accepted to the facility or any sanitary landfill. 
Estimated electricity generation and correspo nding annual estimated emission reduction 
values are 74,235 MWh and 76,729 tCO2 respectively. The starting date of the project activity 
shall be considered as 31/10/2020 which is th e provisional acceptance date of the first two 
gas engines.  
The Ministry of Environment and Urbanization has authorized the Environmental Impact 
Assessment (EIA) Report. According to Turkish ru les and regulations, an EIA's permission may 
only be granted if the project in question has no detrimental effects on the environment or the 
economy, thus the project has no net harm. All kinds of preventive measures will be taken to 
minimize the odor, dust, leachate, gas and similar negative effects that may arise from the 
facility. These preventive measures are explained in Environmental Impact Assessment Report 
in further detail.

6.3 Monitoring Plan 
The responsible entity for the monitoring system is Biotrend personnel. The monitoring activities 
will primarily involve four type s of personnel: the Operation Site Manager, Field Operator, 
Electrical & Mechanical Operator  and the Laboratory Operator. 
The Field Operators will perform ac tivities such as monitoring and adjusting LFG extraction wells, 
checking operations of the blower and flare, recording data at the blower/flare station, routine 
maintenance of collection system components, preparing daily logs and completing checklists, 
and send data to the Operation Site Manager. 
The Operation Manager’s responsibilities include reviewing the data collected both manually by 
the Field Technicians and the one recorded automatically by analytical equipment, making 
recommendations and/or implem enting system adjustments to  maximize methane capture and 
destruction, scheduling monitoring and O&M activities, performing quality assurance checks on 
operations, coordinating with system component manufacturers as needed , to maintain proper 
operations and calibration, and compiling data as required by the Methodology. 
Only for manual data collection, the Operation Manager will be responsible for reviewing the data 
collected. 
Project Management Responsibility 
The operators of the LFG recovery and electricit y generation system will be responsible for 
collecting all data monitored on -site. The operating and mainte nance personnel will be skilled 
technicians, with extensive experience in equipment operation, maintenance and calibration, and 
emergency procedures. Overall responsibility for the monitoring and maintenance of all required 
tasks and their adequate management lie with  the project manager. Detailed roles and 
responsibilities of the relevant staff involved in VCS monitoring are placed.
