1.1 Summary Description of the Project 
İzmir Metropolitan Municipality has implemented the İzmir Integrated Waste Management 
Plan to manage municipal solid waste generated in all of its districts in 20 18. The 
Metropolitan Municipality determined five sub- management regions within the Integrated 
Waste Management Plan's scope. The "Güney (south) -1 Sub-Management Region" where 
this Project serves consists of 7 districts in total, namely Ödemiş, Kiraz, Beydağ, Bayındır, 
Tire, Selcuk and Torbalı. In line with  this Management Plan, İzmir Metropolitan Municipality 
constructed an Integrated Solid Waste Treatment plant in Ödemiş District to treat the 
generated waste in these seven distri cts.  
İzmir Metropolitan Municipality has appointed B iosun Kati Atik Isleme Enerji ve Cevre San.  
Tic. A.S. (hereafter referred to as the  Project Proponent ) to construct and operate  Ödemiş 
Integrated Solid Waste Management  Plant for 29 years in Ödemiş District, İzmir Province, 
Turkey. Different waste treatment processes are applied to the incoming fresh waste in the 
Plant. Ödemiş Integrated Solid Waste Management Plant (hereafter referred to as Ödemiş 
ISWMP) includes Mechanical /Thermal Treatment  to produce Refuse-Derived Fuel (RDF) 
and Anaerobic Digestion with Biogas Recove ry. Ödemiş ISWMP aims to regain packaging 
and biodegradable wastes for the economy, generate energy, and direct resid ual wastes to 
landfills. In the Plant, packaging wastes and valuable metals are recycled, methane gas is 
obtained from biodegradable wastes by anaerobic digestion and then converted into 
electricity and heat in the cogeneration plant. Some portion of the digestate from the 
anaerobic digesters is treated further to produce organic fertili ser, and the remaining part is 
directed to the thermal drying unit , where the moisture content of the digestate  is reduced. 
The portion of digestate with reduced moisture content is then directed to the landfill.  
The Ödemis ISWMP accepts a daily mixed fresh waste of 845 tons/day. The incoming mixed 
waste is first treated in the Material Recovery Facility , where recyclable, biodegradable, 
bulky, combustible, and residual wastes are separated. Biodegradable wastes resulting from 
Material Recovery Facility  are mixed with the separately collected biodegradables, 
agricultural&forestry wastes from gardens and parks, and animal farms (i.e. animal manure). 
The mixed biodegradable wastes  are then forwarded to the anaerobic digesters , where a 
total daily waste amount of 800 tons is processed.  The biogas that is released during the 
biodegradation of organic wastes in anaerobic digesters is used for electricity and heat 
production in the cogeneration unit.  
On 03/10/2020, EMRA granted the Pr oject activity with a Generation License of 20.28 MWe 
for 27 years. The License is granted for the Plant to have 8 digesters and 13 gas engines  
for a capacity of 20.28 MWe. The plant's design also considered an incoming waste amount

are provided with collection containers for mixed waste and recyclables. Waste collection 
vehicles are used to collect mixed waste and recyclables from containers. The collected 
waste is brought to the transfer stations located in Çaylı, Torbalı , Kısıkköy and Selcuk 
Transfer Stations, and from here, waste is transferred to the Ödemiş ISWMP to be treated.

Material Recovery Facility  
The Material Recovery Facility  consists of easily operational and maintainable sorting 
machinery with a design capacity of 718 tons per day. The basic process flow of the Material 
Recovery Facility consists of waste reception leading to a feeding bunker & bag opener by 
means of a wheeled loader. After the bag opener, the fresh waste is forwarded to a trommel 
screen. Trommel screens are the machines which separate the waste according to 
predetermined size s. This process occurs with the help of the drum inside of the trommel 
screen, which has holes with a specific diameter. The three different sizes of waste are 
obtained from the trommel screen:  the waste with a particle size greater than 280 mm, the 
waste with a particle size between 140-280 mm and the waste with a particle size of 0-140 
mm. The recyclable part of waste with a particle size greater than 280 mm is recovered, and 
the non-recyclables are sent to the landfill.  
The rejects with a diameter of 0- 140 mm from the trommel screen are then processed 
towards the finger screens . Finger screen s are named after finger -shaped steel pipes. 
Springs in the finger screen separate the material via vibration into two different 
sizes. Rejects with a smaller particle size from the finger screen are then processed towards 
the magnetic separator. Magnets are used to separate ferrous materials from the rest of the 
refuse. A belt magnet is installed above a conveyor belt, with the belt on the magnet moving 
across the co nveyor carrying the refuse. And after magnets the wastes are processed 
towards the Biogas Plant.  
The rejects with a diameter of 70- 280 mm from the trommel screen are then processed 
towards a ballistic separator. A ballistic separator is a mechanical sorting device with 
oscillating paddles. Alternating sets of paddles provide agitation of the material stream. The 
agitation has a different effect on the mixed waste stream, causing a separation of rigid items 
off one end and flexible items off the other. After  the ballistic separator, magnets and optical 
sorters are employed to recover glass, plastics and ferrous metals. Moreover, baling presses 
are used in the Plant to bale materials such as Paper, paper, Nylon, Plastic, wood, sack, tin, 
fibre, and textile was te. All non -recyclable combustible wastes are then forwarded to the

Biogas Plant (Anaerobic Digesters)  
Three anaerobic digesters operate  in the biogas plant with 400 tons/day capacit y. The 
reactors are made from reinforced concrete, leak-proof, and designed to have a self-heating 
and mixing system.  Three different waste reception units are employed in the Plant for 
biodegradable wastes from the mechanical treatment, animal manure collected from the 
animal farms and the separately collected garden park wastes. The different types of 
biodegradable wastes are then homogeni sed in the unit to be fed to the anaerobic digester. 
There are two separate homogeni sation tanks in the biogas plant.  Biodegradable wastes 
from the Material Recovery Facility  and separately collected biodegradable wastes, animal 
manure, agricultural&forestry wastes are accepted to all three anaerobic digesters after 
homogenisation. Biodegradable waste is then digested by microorganisms under  anaerobic 
conditions and converted into carbon dioxide and methane gas. On average, the hydrauli c 
retention time of the waste in the digesters is 20 days. In the digester, methane gas is 
produced under mesophilic conditions at 38 degrees Celsius; the methane gas produced is 
withdrawn by blowers  from the digester and deli vered to the cogeneration unit  to obtain 
electrical and thermal energy. Hot water pipes placed inside the digesters provide automatic

control of the temperature of the digester to keep it constant with continuous hot water 
circulation. 
Also, ten mixers inside the digesters are activated according to the time parameters 
determined in the Plant's automation system to keep the mixture homogenous.   
Before starting the project activity (in the baseline scenario), manure from the farms was 
being washed out from underneath the animal barns and stored in anaerobic lagoons, which 
allowed the manure to decay and emit methane into the atmosphere along with several other 
toxic gases. Such applications also bear the risk of lagoon overflow due to increased 
rainfalls, strong Biogas or improper constru ction. 
Energy requirements for the Plant will be supplied from the national grid, and a generator 
will be ready to use in an emergency electricity need.

desulphurisation unit, the produced biogas is stored in double membrane inflatable roofs

Cogeneration Unit and Flare Unit  
The CHP unit has  five electricity -generating engines , each with a capacity of 1.56 MWe 
totalling up to 7.80 MWe. Biogas energy fed to gas engines will be converted into electricity 
with 42.1% efficiency and thermal energy with 42.1 % efficiency. All the electricity produced 
is supplied to the grid, which also meets the electricity consumed by the Plant. The total 
efficiency of the gas engine s used is over 84%. There is also a flare unit with a capacity of 
6000m3/hr in t he biogas plant for emergenc ies where the engines don't combust produced 
biogas. The combustion temperature of the flare is around 800 -9000C, and the flare's 
efficiency is higher than 95%.  
The heat generated in the CHP plant is used to heat the anaerobic digesters, the 
administrative building, and the  thermal drying unit in the Refuse- derived fuel plant. 
Hygienization and Dewatering Unit 
 The digestate from the digester s is sent to the dewatering unit. The digestate with a dry 
matter of 20% is increased to  40% at the dewatering unit . And the dry matter content of the  
remaining digestate is reduced to less than 10% producing a liquid fertili ser. The solid 
fertiliser from the dewatering unit is then subjected to the hygienization pr ocess at a 
temperature of 7 0 degrees for 1 hour per the regulation and then sent to the landfill. The 
liquid fertiliser from the dewatering unit is stored in a closed equalisation tank with a capacity 
of 500 tons and recycled back to the digesters. In the later stages, the excess liquid fertiliser 
will be subjected to pre-treatment in the Plant's wastewater treatment plant to be established 
and discharged to the İzmir Metropolitan Municipality's wastewater treatment plant . 
Refuse-derived Fuel Plant  
The non-recyclable, combustible wastes sorted in the Material Recovery Facility  are utilised 
in the RDF Plant. The wastes utilised in the RDF Plant include textile wastes, fibre, surface 
coating materials, diapers, pads, sawdust, splinters, wood, plywood, etc. The RDF Plant's 
capacity is designed considering an incoming amount of 476  tons/day of waste.  
Part of the solid fertiliser produced in the biogas plant is further dried and treated in the RDF 
plant. Thus its calorific value can be increased to be sent to the cement factories for 
incineration. The wastes to be used as RDF is first reduced in size by shredders and dried 
in the RDF plant. According to the waste characterisation study conducted by the 
Municipality, combustible wastes have a percentage of %25.30 of all the incoming waste to 
the ISWMP . The residual waste resulting from the RDF Plant is sent to the landfill.

1.17 Sustainable Development Contributions 
 Sustainable Development Contributions Activity Description 
SDG 13 Climate Action 
The Project Activity results in emissions reduction by capturing the methane in the municipal 
solid waste and converting it to electricity and heat in the cogeneration plant. Without the 
Project Activity, the municipal solid waste generated would be left to decay in a landfill, 
producing landfill gas with a partial capture of the LFG and flaring the captured LFG. By 
avoiding methane emissions, the project activity will result in an average emission reduction 
of 139,044 tCO2e annually. Additionally, in the cogeneration plant, captured methane is 
converted to electricity and heat, where the generated electricity is supplied to the grid, and 
the generated heat is used for heating the buildings and in the thermal drying unit to dewater 
the solid fertiliser obtained. Without the Project Activity, the electricity generated would be 
supplied from the national grid where thermal power plants are connected, causing more 
GHG emissions. The heat necessary for the building and drying purposes would be provided 
by the fossil fuels used, causing further GHG emissions.  
Additionally, the ISWMP accepts animal manure from nearby farms. Without the Project 
Activity, the animal manure generated in the farms would be left to decay in an anaerobic 
lagoon producing methane gas. With the Project Activity, the collected animal manure is 
being used in the digesters to produce biogas and energy, avoiding the release of methane 
into the atmosphere along with several other toxic gases.  
Moreover, the non-recyclable and combustible part of the incoming waste will be revalued in 
the ISWMP as refuse-derived fuel and sent to the cement factories to replace the fossil fuel 
usage, thus preventing the GHG emissions resulting from fossil fuel combustion.  
SDG 7 Affordable and Clean Energy  
The Project Activity results in renewable energy generation by digesting municipal solid 
waste and animal manure in an anaerobic environment to produce electricity and heat. The 
baseline scenario for using gas generated from an anaerobic digester (biogas) is that the 
electricity delivered by the Project to the National Grid would have otherwise been generated 
by the operation of grid- connected power plants and/or by the addition of new generation 
sources into the grid. Turkish electricity generation is main ly composed of thermal power 
plants, and the share of fossil fuel  resources in increasing energy demand of the country ; 
has also increased  significantly in recent years. In the absence of the proposed project 
activity, the same amount of electricity is required to be supplied via either the existing power 
plants or by increasing the number of thermal power plants, thus increasing GHG emissions. 
The clean energy generated by the Project Activity is calculated based on the electricity 
generated per annum. The Project is expected to generat e 32,307.6 MWh of clean energy 
annually and contributes to the share of renewable energy sources.

Leakage Management 
The floor of the waste reception unit is made of 30 cm thick, C30 concrete and non-

constructed at the base of the waste reception unit to prevent the waste from contacting or

for leachate on the Material Recovery Facility floor, and grate channels are used to collect 
the leachate.  Moreover, a leachate storage tank is employed in the Plant to transfer the 
collected leachate to the wastewater treatment plant of İzmir Metropolitan Municipal ity.

2.3 Environmental Impact 
Within the scope of the environmental impact assessment study carried out for the project 
activity, the following key environmental impacts were assessed . 
Air Pollution 
A detailed study has been carried out within the scope of EIA regarding the emissions 
expected to occur during the construction and operation periods of the project activity.  
Electricity is used as an energy source in Plant units, and there are emissions fro m waste 
transportation activities to the material recovery facility and biogas plant. These emission 
amounts are calculated in the scope of EIA.  Based on the resulting calculations, it was 
determined that the emissions to be generated are below the regulat ory limit values. Biogas 
filters and biogas treatment units are employed in the biogas plant to ensure that pollutant 
emissions in the plant are kept well below the regulatory thresholds.  
Water Pollution 
Most liquid fertiliser obtained from the biogas plan t is recycled back into the system. The 
remaining liquid fertiliser product formed in the Biogas Plant is supplied to the market as

Organic, Mineral and Microbial Source Fertilizers Used in Agriculture". However, suppose 
the liquid fertiliser product does not meet the fert iliser criteria. In that case, t he pollutant 
levels will be reduced to the desired levels in the pre- treatment unit constructed in the 
ISWMP and then sent to the wastewater treatment plant under the responsibility of Izmir 
Metropolitan Municipality of Turkey by sewage trucks. At every stage of the Project, the 
provisions of the "Water Pollution Control Regulation", which entered into force after b eing 
published in the Official Gazette dated 31.12.2004 and numbered 25687, will be complied 
with.

The Project aims to minimise the adverse effects of inadequate waste management 
practices and use solid wastes as a resource. Biodegradable, recyclable, and residual 
wastes are obtained from the Material Recovery Facility . Biodegradable wastes are sent to 
the Biogas Plant, and recyclable wastes are then pressed and shipped to licensed 
recovery/recycling companies. The remaining waste that cannot be recovered is sent to the 
landfill. Recyclable wastes generated during the construction and operation phases of  the

Assessment of likelihood conditions for each parameter to reach benchmark IRR is provided 
below: 
Electricity Price  
To reach the 14.5 % benchmark IRR, electricity prices should increase more than 10 % from 
the assumed price. Even if the electricity price increases by 10%, project IRR reaches 
11.48%, still lower than the benchmark value of 14.5%.  
Investment Cost  
In order to reach the benchmark IRR, investment costs shall be decreased by more than 
10%. Since the equipment contract , which has a higher share of the total costs , is fixed, a 
10% decrease in the investment cost is unlikely. Thus, it is not likely for project activity to 
have a threshold investment cost and reach to benchmark IRR. Even if investment cost 
decreases by 10%, project IRR reaches 11.65%, still lower than the benchmark value of  
14.5%.

Data Processing and collection  
The monitoring equipment and other plant equipment are connected to a Programmable 
Logic Controller (PLC), which collects all the monitoring data and sends them to the SCADA 
system so that the operator can read the main parameters in real -time. The PLC archives 
reading values in a database which is accessible through the SCADA interface. Monitored 
data can be read and downloaded with the SCADA system for reporting purposes. Moreover, 
these electronic records are backed -up in a separate computer. The main source for landfill 
gas data is the Project activity's PLC -SCADA system. The system automatically reads 
measurement devices and records data on hard drives. The PLC system records meter 
readings at pre-determined intervals, as specified in the CDM monitoring manual. This data 
is used to calculate the total emission reductions.  
1) Flow measurements  
Gas flow is measured by flow meters, including the flow into the flare, generator s and the 
total flow. These parameters are required to be normalised at 0°C and 1 atm, using actual 
gas temperature and pressure data to be measured with temperature and pressure 
transmitters. These in- built transmitters are integrated into each flow meter  and equipped 
with a flow computer so that the values sent to the SCADA system have already been 
normalised (converted to normal cubic meters). Therefore, temperature and pressure data 
at each flowmeter do not need to be separately monitored. Methane content in the gas is 
measured by a gas analyser connected to the PLC -SCADA system. 
Calibration: The flow sensors are calibrated according to the gas's specified temperature, 
pressure, and composition as per the manufacturer's recommendation. The equipment 
selected will allow dynamic compensation for these parameters, normalised to standard 
temperature, pressure, and gas composition. There will be a periodic verification according 
to the requirement of equipment specifications.  
2) Gas Quality and efficiency of the flare 
An enclosed flare unit determines methane destruction with an inbuilt flame detection unit.
